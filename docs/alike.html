Look Alike Analysis



Let's talk about look alike modelling, and no, it has nothing to do with your appearance! Look alike modelling is a technique that a company can use to find potential customers for their products. How do they do this? Well, from the data they collect about you and others online. Companies have the ability to figure out plenty of different kinds of information about you from your gender to what smartphone you use. This helps companies find better customers and advertise to them in hopes that they spend money on them. If they want to sell an iPhone case for example, they will gather data on people who own an iPhone and try to market to them specifically. Maybe the product is something targeting a certain group of people, such as men or women. Companies can figure out which person is likely a man or woman and advertise to them. It's a great strategy for advertisers to use if they want to sell to more customers, but at the cost of breaching people's privacy. Sure, maybe you did want to buy that new iPhone case, but is it really worth it at the cost of having some random company have data about you? These are some of the questions we need to ask ourselves about data privacy while on the internet. It's no surprise you sometimes come across ads online that might seem oddly specific to you. It's of course because these companies have a ton of data on you!
